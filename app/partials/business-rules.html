Kata16: Business rules

The problem of business rules have been around for a long time and what I have seen in my past commercial experience leaves a lot to be desired. If I were the business rules designer, here are some of the things that can manage a large set of changing business rules:

Components

  • Development environment: Allow developers to create rules
  • Rules engine and repository: Makes the business decisions. This is deployed in its own environment and exposed as a service to be accessed by different applications.
  • Rules management UI: Web interface that allows non IT users to maintain rules. Rules may be represented by pseudo code and visual UIs for ease of adminstration.

Separation from application code

The core concept. Business rules should exist on its own and not tied to any application calling it.

Interfaces

Separation also goes for activities that are initiated by workflows. The rule engine will know nothing about the applications' implementation of the service interfaces.

Identifying and categorising business rules

  • Is the rule a fact (eg, payment must be for more than $0) or a workflow (eg, successful payment intiatates shipping)
  • Conditions which the rule fires
  • Data that is required to execute the rule

Choice of rules framework

Some things worth considering may be:
  • Fit for the areas of expertise of developers
  • Ease of interfacing with existing systems / services
  • Adheres to industry standards (somewhat, since there is no hard industry standard for rules engines and BPM)
  • Technical skills of non IT users who will maintain the system
  • Price
